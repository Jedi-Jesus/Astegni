## 1. Problem Statement

### 1.1 The Ethiopian Context (Current Market)

Ethiopia has a massive private tutoring and skills training market, but it operates in complete chaos:

The Market Reality:
- Parents widely hire private tutors for their children's academic subjects (math, science, English, etc.)
- Individuals widely hire instructors to excel in both academic AND non-academic programs (music, culinary arts, cosmetology, languages, arts, sports, vocational training, university courses, certification programs, etc.)
- This is a multi-million dollar market with zero infrastructure for quality control, transparency, or trust

The Problem: Finding quality tutors and instructors is a nightmare.

---

#### Current Pain Points for Individuals and Parents Looking for Both Academic and Non-Academic Subjects:

Method #1: Billboards on Electric Poles - The primary offline method in Ethiopia
- No vetting, no reviews, no background checks
- Just a phone number scribbled on a pole
- Parents/students have no idea who they're hiring
- Risk: Trusting a stranger with your education based on a poster
- Who uses this: Mostly parents seeking academic tutors for children, some individuals seeking music/arts instructors

Method #2: Unverified Agents and Brokers - Middlemen claiming to provide tutors
- No transparency (agents take hidden cuts, tutors don't know)
- No accountability (if tutor is bad, agent disappears)
- No quality control (agents just want commissions)
- Result: Parents/students pay more, tutors earn less, quality suffers
- Who uses this: Parents seeking academic tutors, individuals seeking professional skills (languages, cooking, etc.)

Method #3: Social Media Channels - WhatsApp/Telegram groups
- Unverified claims ("I'm the best math tutor!" "Expert guitar teacher!")
- No rating system, no reviews, no proof of expertise
- Same tutors spam multiple groups with identical messages
- Problem: Impossible to distinguish good tutors from frauds
- Who uses this: Everyone - parents, students, individuals seeking both academic and non-academic instruction

Method #4: YouTube - The go-to online option for learning
- ‚ùå One-sided communication: Only the instructor explains, students can't ask questions in real-time
- ‚ùå No personalized feedback: Can't get help when stuck on a specific technique or concept
- ‚ùå Comment section is useless:
  - When questions ARE answered: Days or weeks later (time lost, momentum lost, already gave up)
  - When questions AREN'T answered: Most of the time - forcing students to search through multiple videos hoping to find the answer (hours wasted)
- ‚ùå No way to connect: Even if you love the instructor and want in-person or one-on-one online tutoring, it's impossible to hire them
  - Instructor doesn't respond to comments asking for private lessons
  - No booking system, no contact information, no way to reach them
- Who uses this: Everyone - parents looking for supplemental academic help for kids, students seeking academic courses, individuals learning non-academic skills (music, cooking, beauty, sports, languages, etc.)

The Monetization Gap:
- üîá Tutors/Instructors Can't Monetize Their Expertise:
  - YouTube content creators want to offer paid personalized sessions (online or in-person)
  - Students and parents want to hire their favorite YouTube instructors for one-on-one help
  - There's no platform to connect them - massive lost opportunity for both sides

The Universal Result:
- Parents: Forced to accept whoever is available for their children, blindly trusting strangers with their child's education and money
- Individuals: Struggle alone with YouTube videos or unverified tutors, unable to ask questions, get feedback, or hire instructors they trust

---

#### The Core Problems (Universal Across Academic and Non-Academic):

| Problem | Impact | Who It Hurts |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| No Quality Verification | Parents/students hire unqualified tutors blindly | Students receive poor education, waste time |
| No Ratings or Reviews | Impossible to know if tutor is good before hiring | Wasted money on bad tutors, damaged trust |

This is a broken market desperate for a solution.

|---------------|------------------|----------------|
